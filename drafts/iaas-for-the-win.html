IaaS For The Win

Tweet
Mon 25 May 2015

A few years ago shared hosting was the norm for deploying most applications, very few people had the resources to deploy anything they wanted without restrictions.

In the shared hosting model you had to pay accordingly to the platform you wanted (PHP being the cheapest among all), so the choice of a platform was heavily influenced by its deploying costs, hence Java and its pricey fashion "Enterprise Ready (TM)".

This model is called PaaS: Platform as a Service.

With the rise of virtualization technologies (containers as well as full virt), hosting providers like Linode, Digital Ocean, Vultr and others offer enough power to deploy anything runnable on a Linux server.
