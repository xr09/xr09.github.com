PHP: The Elephant On The Room

Tweet
Thu 27 March 2014

Why PHP?

In one word: hosting.

There's an emerging platform I'd love to deploy some apps in, but there is 90% probabilities the thing will be PHP only.

Right now there's struggle in my head trying to find a PHP framework which doesn't sucks (compared to Django and Flask).

What do I want?

  • Templating engine: I like Symfony's Twig, Laravel's Blade and Phalcon's Volt.

Phalcon is out of my reach, is not likely that a new hosting platform would let a user install C extensions.

  • ORM: I like Propel, Doctrine is not my favorite, and Eloquent looks fine.

With some Symfony experience under my belt, it seems like the most obvoius choice. Twig is a big plus,

But Symfony is somewhat bloated from my POV, doctrine, xml schemas, so many config files, the thing grew up commit by commit and you need like 6 bundles minimum for any project. Is

First of, I like explicit frameworks, don't give me too much magic, although magic seems nice at first, but when you try to invoke some advanced trick the bunny may get lost in the hat.
